Historic Space Missions

Sputnik 1 (USSR, 1957)

Sputnik 1 was the world's first artificial satellite. Launched by the Soviet Union, it marked the beginning of the Space Age and sparked the Space Race between the USSR and the United States.

Apollo 11 (USA, 1969)

Apollo 11 became the first mission to land humans on the Moon. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked on the lunar surface while Michael Collins remained in orbit aboard the command module.

Voyager 1 (USA, 1977)

Voyager 1 explored the outer planets and later became the first human-made object to enter interstellar space. It continues to send data back to Earth today.

Mars Pathfinder (USA, 1997)

Mars Pathfinder successfully landed on Mars and deployed the Sojourner rover, proving that robotic vehicles could operate on another planet.

International Space Station (1998–Present)

The ISS is the largest human-made structure in space and has been continuously inhabited since 2000. It serves as a laboratory for international scientific research.

Chandrayaan-1 (India, 2008)

Chandrayaan-1 was India's first lunar mission. It played a major role in confirming the presence of water molecules on the Moon, one of the most important lunar discoveries of the century.

Mangalyaan / Mars Orbiter Mission (India, 2013)

India became the first nation to reach Mars orbit on its first attempt. Mangalyaan demonstrated ISRO's engineering capabilities at an exceptionally low mission cost.

Rosetta (Europe, 2014)

The European Space Agency's Rosetta mission became the first spacecraft to orbit and land a probe on a comet, helping scientists understand the origins of the Solar System.

Chandrayaan-3 (India, 2023)

Chandrayaan-3 successfully achieved a soft landing near the Moon's south pole. India became the fourth country to land on the Moon and the first to reach the lunar south polar region.

Artemis Program (USA, Present)

NASA's Artemis program aims to return humans to the Moon, establish a sustainable lunar presence, and prepare for future missions to Mars.
